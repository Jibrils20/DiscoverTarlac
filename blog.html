Why Tarlac is so special?

Title description, Dec 7, 2017

Heres Why.

Tarlac is the province in Central Luzon with the most diverse population. This province is home to four separate ethnic groups: Pampangos, Ilocanos, Pangasinenses, and Tagalogs. Living together has resulted in bringing visitors the greatest food of their ancestors' homelands, namely Bulacan, Nueva Ecija, Zambales, Pangasinan, and the Ilocos Region. Tarlac is also noted for its excellent cuisine and extensive sugar and rice farms. The fact that it is the melting pot of Central Luzon contributes to its excellent culinary.Tarlac is one of the best destinations to visit in Central Luzon because of its historical landmarks, fine cuisine, extensive sugar and rice plantations, and a wonderfully groomed golf course, among other things.


Brief History

Title description, Sep 2, 2017

Did you know ?..

The name "Tarlac" comes from the Aeta word "Malatarlak," which refers to a "talahib-like" weed found in the western mountain regions of what is now the municipality of Bamban. The Spaniards formed the final province in Central Luzon, Tarlac, in 1874. It was formed from the provinces of Pampanga and Pangasinan, which explains the area's two major languages, Kapampangan (spoken by more than half of the population) and Ilocano (spoken by the rest) (roughly 41 percent )
